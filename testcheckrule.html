How to Calculate Typing Speed & Accuracy for PSSSB Typing Exams

This page explains step-by-step how Gross Speed, Accuracy, and Net Typing Speed are calculated in Government Typing Tests such as PSSSB Clerk / DEO Typing Exams.

1. What is a “Word” in Typing Tests?

1 Word = 5 Characters (including spaces & punctuation)

Example: If you type 250 characters:

250 ÷ 5 = 50 Words

2. Gross Typing Speed (WPM)

Gross speed means typing speed without deducting mistakes.

Formula:
Gross WPM = (Total Characters Typed ÷ 5) ÷ Time in Minutes

Example:

  • Total Characters Typed = 1200
  • Time = 10 minutes
  • 1200 ÷ 5 = 240 words
  • 240 ÷ 10 = 24 WPM

3. Accuracy Calculation

Mistakes include wrong words, missing words, spelling mistakes, punctuation errors, etc.

Accuracy % = (Correct Words ÷ Total Words Typed) × 100

Example:

  • Total Words = 240
  • Errors = 12
  • Correct Words = 240 − 12 = 228
  • Accuracy = (228 ÷ 240) × 100 = 95%

4. Net Typing Speed (Final WPM)

This is the final valid speed after deducting mistakes.

Net WPM = Gross WPM − (Errors ÷ Time in Minutes)

Example:

  • Gross WPM = 24
  • Errors = 12
  • Time = 10 minutes
  • Errors per minute = 12 ÷ 10 = 1.2
  • Net WPM = 24 − 1.2 = 22.8 WPM
